## How to Maximize Your 90 Days

### Week 1-4: Foundation

- Choose ONE skill to focus on

- Complete foundational courses/training

- Set up necessary tools and accounts

- Practice daily for at least 2-3 hours

### Week 5-8: Skill Development

- Work on real or practice projects

- Join relevant online communities

- Study successful practitioners

- Get feedback on your work

### Week 9-11: Portfolio Building

- Create 3-5 strong portfolio pieces

- Document your process and results

- Build a simple portfolio website or page

- Gather testimonials (even from pro bono work)

### Week 12-13: Launch

- Set your rates

- Create service packages

- Start outreach (50+ contacts)

- Join freelance platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, etc.)

## Success Tips

**1. Focus on One Skill:** Don't try to learn multiple skills simultaneously. Master one, earn money, then expand.

**2. Practice Daily:** Consistency beats intensity. 2-3 hours daily is better than 20 hours on weekends.

**3. Build in Public:** Share your learning journey on LinkedIn or Twitter to build credibility and attract early clients.

**4. Start Before You're Ready:** Your first client doesn't need you to be an expert, just better than they are.

**5. Undercharge Initially:** Your first 3-5 clients are your real education. Charge enough to be taken seriously but focus on results and testimonials.

**6. Specialize:** "Social media manager for dentists" is more valuable than "social media manager."

## Getting Your First Clients

- **Your Network:** Let everyone know your new skill

- **Free Value:** Share tips and insights on LinkedIn/Twitter

- **Cold Outreach:** Email 10 businesses daily who need your service

- **Freelance Platforms:** Create compelling profiles on Upwork, Fiverr

- **Pro Bono/Discount:** Offer first 2-3 clients a discount for testimonials

- **Local Businesses:** Walk into local shops and offer your services

## Final Thoughts

These skills are genuinely learnable in 90 days with focused effort, but remember: learning the skill is only half the battle. The other half is learning to sell yourself, communicate with clients, and deliver consistent results. Your first $1,000 month might take 90 days, but your first $5,000+ month is often just 3-6 months after that once you have momentum and proof of work.
